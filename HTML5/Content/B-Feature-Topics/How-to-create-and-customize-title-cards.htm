Topic 3: How to create and customize title cards

Title cards are text displayed on the screen to convey information to the watcher. In this way, they serve a similar purpose to chapters but may also introduce the video’s title, provide context, or separate individual segments of a video. ClipChamp’s Editor application can create and/or edit title cards to address different needs.

Create a title card in ClipChamp Editor

Click on “Open editor.”

ClipChamp’s built-in editing software opens.

[Alt text=”open editor option in ClipChamp player page”]

Click on “Text” on the left side menu.

Editor opens a menu you can scroll through with different text options, including text with different styles.

[Alt text=”red circle around text option on the side menu”]

Select one option and drag it over to the timeline.

Place the text above where you want it to appear in the video.

[Alt text=”the text above the timeline where it was placed’]

A textbox appears in the preview.

Click inside the text box in the preview.

[Alt text=”highlighted text to be changed by user”]

Type in whatever you want the text to say.

Change the font of the chapter card

Click on the title from the timeline.

The text property menu opens on the right where you can customize the text

[Alt text=”the text property menu with options such as font and advanced settings”]

.Click on the dropdown arrow beside the font name.

A scrollable dropdown menu opens with different font choices, including headings, subheadings, and body text.

[Alt text=”a scrollable panel of font choices”]

Select the desired font.

After selecting a choice, the text will automatically change to the chosen font.

Adjust the alignment of the text

Open the text property menu.

Click on the “alignment” icon from the text property menu.

[Alt text=”the text property menu with three alignment options: left, center, and right]

Select from the three options: Left, Center, or Right

The text within the box will follow the alignment you choose.

Change the font size/general size of the text

Navigate to the preview and click on the text.

[Alt text=”the text surrounded by an interactable textbox”]

Click and drag the corners of the text box to adjust its size.

[Alt text=”The text adjusted to the new textbox size”]

Change the color of the text

Navigate to the “Color” section of the text property menu.

Depending on what text/text style you selected, the default color will be different.

[Alt text=”The color section under the font section]

Click on the colored circle in the menu.

Depending on the text/text style you selected, the color options offered will be different.

[Alt text=”color options coming out from the colored circle”]

Click on the desired color or type in a HEX value.

The text will automatically change to the color you choose.
