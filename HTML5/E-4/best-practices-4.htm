What are the best practice when creating title cards?

Make it readable!

Ensure your title cards have enough contrast to be legible. Best practice is white or light text on a black, or dark-colored, solid background. The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WGAC) requires that all text set to a size of eighteen-points or larger must have a contrast ratio minimum of 3:1 (which means the lighter of the two colors is exactly three times as bright as the darker one). If you’re unsure about your contrast, you can use the WebAIm Contrast Checker!

Make sure your font is easy to read. Accessible fonts are typically medium-weight, sans-serif, such as Arial or Helvetica. These fonts are easier for people with visual impairments or dyslexia to read because they reduce visual noise. There are also fonts specifically designed for low vision and readability: Lexend and Atkinson Hyberlegible. Your font should also be large enough to read. For a title card, aim for a minimum of eighteen-point font (WGAC).

Make it useful!

The whole point of a title card is to allow viewers to easily jump around a video, and gain a quick understanding about the video’s content.

Keep it concise: people should be able to read it quickly.

But also, keep it descriptive. Your title card should still give the viewer a pretty good understanding on what that section is about, or the point you are trying to reiterate. A good way to test this: if someone has not watched your video yet, but saw all your title cards, would they have a good understanding of what takes place in your video?
