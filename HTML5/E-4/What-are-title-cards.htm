What are title cards?

Title cards (also referred to as intertitles) are frames of text inserted directly into a video to provide context and structure. Sometimes title cards work to highlight key information in a video, either through summarizing information or reinforcing main points. They also act as a “directory” for the content of the video by clearly segmenting the video into different parts (kind of like chapters in a book, but displayed clearly on the screen).

Title cards appear as pictures or frames throughout the video.

A popular use of title cards was in silent films. They provided dialogue and context for which there was no audio.

Title cards are a great way to give your video more structure for viewers, and are a simple way to make your content both memorable and enjoyable for your audience.

How do title cards improve digital accessibility?

Title cards provide context throughout a video, and are a great example of design that helps people with disabilities, and creates an overall better user experience for all viewers. When implementing digital accessibility, we want to ensure that all people are able to understand our content. When making videos, it’s important to provide the most context and organization possible. Title cards are a great way to do this.

Title cards are a great way to aid deaf users or those hard of hearing with important context throughout the video. By clearly labelling different sections, or taking a moment to summarize key points, title cards allow a “pause” in the script. This can be useful for those with hearing impairments reading dialogue, rather than listening to a regular rhythm of speech. Title cards are also useful for those watching a video in a loud environment by providing clear and distinct markers throughout the video in case they missed something, or need a refresh.

Title cards can also help those with cognitive disabilities by reinforcing main points, therefore making for better retention of information. Studies show audio only information has a retention rate of 10% after 72 hours, but combining it with visual content increases the retention rate to 65% in the same period of time. Title cards can help those with cognitive disabilities by providing mental “breadcrumbs” of the video. These “breadcrumbs” allow them to get a recap of what they’ve already seen or learned, and what’s coming up.
