What are the best practices for audio description?

When using audio description it is important to keep in mind what information is important, and what can be excluded. Not including important information can lead to confusion, especially for hard of hearing or low vision individuals who may depend on detailed descriptions. It is important to keep your wording neat and descriptive, focusing on need-to-know information. To the extent possible, it is best to use integrated descriptions within the main audio track, which will describe the important visual information without the need of adding audio description.

Tips to keep in mind

Audio description

  • Audio description may not always be necessary in a video if the video is text on a blank background with the narrator reading out the words. For example; a “How To” guide for a website or software.

  • It is best to use a voice and style that is different from the original audio, so that it is distinguishable from any other voices.

  • It is important to put the description just before or at the same time as the visual content, never after. One way to ensure this is by using the audio recorder feature after recording your audio. This will allow you to move your recording to line up with the video however you choose.

Integrated description

  • When using integrated description in instructional videos or information heavy content, avoid using words like “click here”, “see this” or any wording that would require a viewer to have seen what you are doing to understand.

  • It is important to use descriptive language such as “the purple Import button at the top of the screen” or “a new window opens”, wherever possible in the original audio, describing the essential visual cues.

  • You can assume your audience might need some assistive technology to access your content, and it is best to keep many types of disabilities in mind when creating your audio descriptions to be most effective for everyone. See section “Types of Disabilities and Who Benefits from AD?” for more information.
